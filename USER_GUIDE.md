## âš ï¸ 5. Limitations and Considerations

Nature is brilliant, but biological solutions cannot always be copy-pasted into human engineering.

* **Scale Effects (The Square-Cube Law):** What works for an ant (lifting 50x its weight) will mathematically fail if scaled up to the size of a human without changing the structural proportions.
* **Material Properties:** Biological materials (like bone or spider silk) grow and heal. Engineered materials (like steel or concrete) do not. You must account for these differences in your calculations.
* **Environmental Constraints:** A mechanism optimized for underwater movement may be disastrously inefficient in air.

**Ask ALGET critically:** "What are the limitations of scaling up this insect-inspired mechanism to a vehicle size?"
