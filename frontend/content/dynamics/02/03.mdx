## Impulse

Impulse is the integral of force over time:
$$
\boxed{\vec{J} = \int \vec{F} \, dt = \vec{F}_{avg} \cdot \Delta t}
$$

---

## Impulse-Momentum Theorem

$$
\boxed{\vec{J} = \Delta \vec{p} = m\vec{v}_2 - m\vec{v}_1}
$$

> ðŸ’¡ **Meaning**: The impulse applied equals the change in momentum.

---

## Conservation of Momentum

For a system with **no external net force**:
$$
\boxed{\vec{p}_{total,1} = \vec{p}_{total,2}}
$$

$$
m_A v_{A1} + m_B v_{B1} = m_A v_{A2} + m_B v_{B2}
$$

| **Inelastic** | Conserved | Not conserved |
| **Perfectly Inelastic** | Conserved | Max loss (objects stick) |

## Worked Example

### Example 2.4: Collision of Two Carts

**Problem:** Cart A (2 kg) moves at 5 m/s right. Cart B (3 kg) is at rest. They collide and stick together. Find final velocity.

**Solution:**

Conservation of momentum:
$$
m_A v_A + m_B v_B = (m_A + m_B) v_f
$$

$$
(2)(5) + (3)(0) = (2 + 3) v_f
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{10}{5} = \boxed{2 \text{ m/s}}
$$

---

## Summary

- Momentum: $p = mv$ (vector)
- Impulse: $J = F \cdot \Delta t = \Delta p$
- Conservation: If no external force, total p is constant
- Collisions: Momentum always conserved; KE only in elastic
