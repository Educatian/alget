# Work-Energy for Rigid Bodies

> **"For a rigid body: total kinetic energy = translational + rotational."**

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Calculate** rotational kinetic energy
2. **Apply** work-energy to rotating systems
3. **Analyze** rolling motion using energy
4. **Combine** translational and rotational energy

---

## Kinetic Energy of a Rigid Body

$$
\boxed{T = \frac{1}{2}mv_G^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_G\omega^2}
$$

- First term: translational KE
- Second term: rotational KE

---

## Rolling Without Slipping

For a wheel rolling without slipping:

$$
\boxed{v_G = r\omega}
$$

Combined kinetic energy:
$$
T = \frac{1}{2}mv_G^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_G\omega^2 = \frac{1}{2}(I_G + mr^2)\omega^2
$$

---

## Work-Energy Principle

$$
T_1 + V_1 + W_{nc} = T_2 + V_2
$$

where $T$ now includes both translational and rotational parts.

---

## Worked Example

**Problem:** A solid disk (m = 5 kg, r = 0.2 m) rolls down a 2 m high ramp from rest. Find its speed at the bottom.

**Solution:**

Energy conservation:
$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2
$$

For a disk: $I = \frac{1}{2}mr^2$, and $v = r\omega$:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}mr^2)(\frac{v}{r})^2 = \frac{3}{4}mv^2
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{4gh}{3}} = \sqrt{\frac{4(9.81)(2)}{3}} = \boxed{5.11 \text{ m/s}}
$$

---

## Summary

- Total KE = $\frac{1}{2}mv_G^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_G\omega^2$
- Rolling: $v = r\omega$ (constraint)
- Apply work-energy just like particles, but include rotation
