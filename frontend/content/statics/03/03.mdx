## The Basic Idea

The **method of sections** involves:

1. Making an imaginary **cut** through the truss
2. Treating either section as a **rigid body** in equilibrium
3. Using **3 equilibrium equations** to solve for up to 3 unknown member forces

> ğŸ¯ **When to Use**: When you need forces in **specific members** (especially those in the middle) without analyzing every joint.

## Problem-Solving Procedure

### Step 1: Find Support Reactions
Analyze the entire truss first.

### Step 2: Plan the Cut
Draw an imaginary line through the truss that:
- Passes through the **target member(s)**
- Cuts through **no more than 3 members** (usually)
- Divides the truss into two parts

### Step 3: Choose a Section
Analyze the section with **fewer forces** (simpler FBD).

### Step 4: Draw the FBD
Show:
- External forces and reactions on that section
- Internal member forces at the cut (assume tension)

### Step 5: Apply Equilibrium
$$
\sum F_x = 0, \quad \sum F_y = 0, \quad \sum M_O = 0
$$

**Pro tip**: Choose moment points that eliminate unwanted unknowns!

---

## Strategic Moment Points

When taking moments, choose a point where **unknown forces intersect**:

| To Find | Take Moments About |
|---------|-------------------|
| Top chord force | Point on bottom chord |
| Bottom chord force | Point on top chord |
| Diagonal force | Intersection of other two cut members |

## When to Use Each Method

| Situation | Recommended Method |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Need ALL member forces | **Joints** (systematic) |
| Need ONE specific force | **Sections** (direct) |
| Forces in middle of truss | **Sections** |
| Starting point analysis | **Joints** |
| Complex geometry | Combine both |
