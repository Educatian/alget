## What Is a Couple?

A **couple** consists of two forces that are:
- **Equal in magnitude**
- **Opposite in direction**
- **Separated by a perpendicular distance**

$$
\boxed{M = F \cdot d}
$$

where $d$ is the perpendicular distance between the two forces.

## Couple as a Free Vector

Unlike a moment about a specific point, a couple is a **free vector**â€”its effect is the same regardless of where it acts on the body.

| Property | Moment about a Point | Couple |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Depends on point? | Yes | **No** |
| Net force | May be non-zero | **Zero** |
| Effect | Rotation + Translation | **Pure Rotation** |
| Type | Bound vector | **Free vector** |

This means you can:
- Move a couple anywhere on the body
- Add couples algebraically
- Replace couples with other couples of the same magnitude

## Equivalent Force Systems

Two force systems are **equivalent** if they produce:
1. The same **resultant force**: $\sum \vec{F}_1 = \sum \vec{F}_2$
2. The same **resultant moment** about any point: $\sum M_{O,1} = \sum M_{O,2}$

### Moving a Force to a New Point

To move a force from point A to point B:

1. Add the couple $M = F \cdot d$ (where $d$ is the distance from A to B)
2. Apply the force at the new point B

$$
\text{Force at A} \equiv \text{Force at B} + \text{Couple } M
$$
